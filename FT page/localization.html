Why Localization Matters

Make Every User Feel at Home

People around the world use dates, numbers, and currencies differently. Americans write dates as 12/31/2025, while Europeans write 31/12/2025. Some languages read right-to-left. Users expect to see data formatted the way they're used to—anything else feels wrong or confusing.

Our React Grid automatically formats everything for your users' location and language. Switch the language and watch dates, numbers, currencies, and even the reading direction change instantly. Your app will feel native to users anywhere in the world.

LOCALIZATION CAPABILITIES

What You Can Customize

Numbers & Money

Numbers and currencies display exactly how users in each country expect. Decimal points, thousands separators, and currency symbols all change automatically based on location. $1,299.99 in the US becomes 1.299,99€ in Germany.

Best for: Shopping sites, financial apps, and anywhere you show prices or monetary values.

Essential when users from different countries need to understand prices and amounts clearly.

Dates & Times

Dates show in the format users expect—MM/DD/YYYY for Americans, DD/MM/YYYY for Europeans, YYYY/MM/DD for Chinese users. Time can show as 12-hour or 24-hour format. Everything adapts automatically.

Best for: Calendars, scheduling apps, reports—anywhere dates need to be crystal clear.

Critical when date confusion could cause real problems, like booking systems or deadlines.

Right-to-Left Languages

For languages like Arabic and Hebrew that read right-to-left, the entire grid flips automatically. Text aligns correctly, columns move to the right side, and everything mirrors naturally. It just works.

Best for: Apps targeting Middle Eastern or Hebrew-speaking markets.

Must-have when your users read Arabic, Hebrew, Persian, or other right-to-left languages.

Translate Everything

All the text in the grid—column headers, buttons, menus, messages—can be translated to any language. Load a language pack or provide your own translations. Every word can speak your users' language.

Best for: Apps that need to be completely in the user's native language.

Choose this when you want your entire app interface translated, not just the data.

FAQ

Common Questions

How do I turn on localization?
Just set the language code (like 'de-DE' for German or 'zh-CN' for Chinese) and load the language data. The grid handles the rest automatically.
Can I use Arabic or Hebrew?
Yes! Just enable right-to-left mode and the grid automatically flips everything to work perfectly with RTL languages.
Can I change the button and menu text?
Absolutely! You can translate every piece of text in the grid using language files or by providing your own translations.
Does sorting work correctly in different languages?
Yes! The grid sorts text according to each language's rules, so names and words appear in the correct order for that language.
Can users switch languages on the fly?
Yes! Change the language anytime and everything updates instantly—dates, numbers, text, all of it.
What languages are ready to use?
We provide ready-made language files for major languages. You can also create your own for any language you need.
